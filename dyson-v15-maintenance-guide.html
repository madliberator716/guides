Maintenance

What to clean, when to clean it, and exactly how — so your V15 keeps running like new.

At a Glance — Maintenance Schedule
Every Use
Empty the dustbin
When debris reaches the MAX line — or after every session. A full bin chokes airflow and forces the motor to overwork.
Monthly
Wash the filter · Check brush bars · Wipe exterior
Dyson's official recommendation. Filter maintenance is the single biggest factor in sustained suction power.
Every 3 Months
Deep-clean brush bars · Check wand & tool airways · Clean cyclone
Quarterly teardown catches progressive buildup before it affects performance or damages components.
Every 6 Months
Clean laser lens · Inspect brush bar bristles · Battery calibration run
Semi-annual check on optical components, wear items, and battery health.
Annually / As Needed
Replace filter · Replace brush bar · Consider battery replacement
Worn filters and brush bars quietly degrade performance. Replace even if they look okay after heavy use.
Step-by-Step Tasks

Dustbin

Every use — or when debris reaches the MAX line
1
Power off and disconnect from charger.
2
Hold the machine over a bin. Press the red bin release lever — the base drops open and debris falls out.
3
For stubborn debris stuck inside, use a soft dry brush to dislodge it. Do not use water inside the bin unless you dry it completely before reassembly.
4
Click the bin base firmly back into place until it locks.
Pro TipEmpty before it hits the MAX line — overfilling reduces suction and affects the particle count accuracy on the LCD screen.

Filter

Monthly wash · Annual replacement
1
Power off. Twist and pull the purple filter out from the top of the main body.
2
Hold under cold running water and squeeze gently. Rotate and repeat until the water runs clear.
3
Gently tap the filter on the side of the sink to shake out excess water.
4
Air dry for at least 24 hours — upright, large end down, near a fan or open window. Never use a tumble dryer, microwave, or place near heat.
5
Only reinstall once completely dry. A damp filter can damage the motor.
WarningNever use detergent, washing-up liquid, or polishes on the filter. Cold water only.
Pro TipBuy a spare filter and rotate them — wash one while the other is in use so you're never waiting 24 hours to run the vacuum.

Digital Motorbar™ Brush Bar

Monthly check · Quarterly deep clean
1
Power off. Press the cleaner head release button on the end of the wand and detach the head.
2
Use a coin to unlock the end cap — twist and remove. Do not wash the end cap.
3
Pull the brush bar straight out of the cleaner head.
4
Use the included hair screw tool or scissors to cut away tangled hair and fibers. Work systematically along the full length of the bar.
5
Wipe down with a damp lint-free cloth. Do not submerge in water.
6
Check that the brush bar spins freely by hand before reinstalling. If it feels stiff or grinds, inspect for remaining debris.
7
Slide the brush bar back in and press until it clicks into place. Reattach the end cap.
Pro TipThe anti-tangle comb reduces wrap, but long hair still builds up over time. A monthly 30-second check prevents the motor from straining against resistance you can't feel.

Laser Slim Fluffy™ Brush Bar

Monthly wash
1
Power off and detach the Laser Slim Fluffy cleaner head from the wand.
2
Open the cleaner head cover by pressing the release, then slide the brush bar out.
3
Rinse the brush bar under warm running water. This head's soft roller is washable — unlike the Digital Motorbar.
4
Stand it upright to dry for at least 24 hours in good airflow. Completely dry before reinstalling.
5
Wipe the inside of the cleaner head housing with a damp cloth. Let dry before reassembling.

Laser Lens

Every 6 months — or when the laser looks dim
1
Detach the cleaner head from the wand before cleaning the lens. Never clean it while attached to the machine.
2
Use a soft, dry, lint-free cloth to gently wipe the laser lens. Do not use water, sprays, or abrasive materials.
WarningNever look directly into the laser beam. It is a Class 1 laser — safe for general use, but avoid direct eye exposure.

Cyclone & Main Body Airways

Quarterly · As needed when suction drops
1
Power off. Remove the dustbin.
2
Inspect the air inlet on the right side of the main body (just below where the bin joins) for debris or blockages.
3
Wipe the cyclone shroud (the cone-shaped assembly inside) with a dry microfiber cloth. Remove fine dust buildup from the fins.
4
Do not rinse the cyclone with water. Dry cleaning only.
5
Reattach the bin and confirm it locks before powering on.

Wand & Tool Airways

Quarterly · Immediately when the LCD shows a blockage alert
1
Power off. Detach the wand from the main body and remove any attached tools or cleaner heads.
2
Look through both ends of the wand. Use a long, dry, soft brush to dislodge any debris caught inside.
3
Check attachment tools the same way — look through the inlet and outlet of each tool for blockages.
4
Wipe the exterior of the wand with a damp lint-free cloth.
Pro TipIf the motor pulses and cuts out mid-clean, that's the V15's blockage alert. Don't force it — power off, find and clear the blockage, then restart.

Exterior & Connection Points

Monthly wipe-down
1
Power off. Use a damp lint-free cloth to wipe the outer body, wand, and cleaner head housing.
2
Wipe the charging port and battery contacts with a dry cloth — keeping them clean ensures consistent charging.
3
Check the wand and tool connection points for dust or grit that could prevent a firm click-in. Wipe clean and dry.
4
Do not use detergents, polishes, or air fresheners on any part of the machine.

Battery

Ongoing habits · Calibration every 6 months
1
Use Eco or Auto mode for everyday cleaning — Boost mode drains the battery significantly faster and generates more heat.
2
Store the machine in a cool, dry location above 5°C (41°F). The V15 will not charge or operate below that threshold to protect the cells.
3
If you have a click-in spare battery, alternate between them and keep both charged — this balances cycle wear across both packs.
4
Calibration (every 6 months): Fully charge the battery, then run the vacuum until it shuts off. Recharge fully. This recalibrates the runtime estimate shown on the LCD.
5
If runtime has noticeably shortened after 2–3 years of regular use, replace the battery. Dyson click-in batteries are user-replaceable — press the red release button on the handle.
WarningNever use the machine or attempt to charge if the battery feels hot to the touch. Let it cool fully before the next use.

Brush Bar Bristle & Wear Inspection

Every 6 months · Replace when worn
1
Remove the brush bar from either cleaner head (see steps above).
2
Inspect bristles for significant shortening, flattening, or bending. Worn bristles lose agitation power on carpet.
3
Check that the brush bar spins freely by hand. Grinding or stiffness after cleaning out debris means internal wear — replace the cleaner head.
4
Replace the entire cleaner head if bristles are significantly worn. Dyson sells replacement heads — use your serial number to confirm compatibility.
What Not to Do
✗
Never put any part in a dishwasher — heat and detergent will warp plastic and damage seals.
✗
Never reinstall a damp filter — moisture in the motor causes irreversible damage and voids the warranty.
✗
Never use detergents or polishes on any part of the machine, including the filter and bin.
✗
Never vacuum liquids — the V15 is a dry vacuum only. Liquids will damage the motor and cyclone.
✗
Never force attachments — if a tool or head doesn't click in cleanly, check for grit or debris at the connection point before forcing it.
✗
Never clear a blockage while running — power off first. The machine auto-cuts if it detects a blockage, but don't try to reach in while the motor is on.
